A Walk Through the Boroughs of New York


A BRIEF HISTORY

The city’s first coffee importers established themselves in lower Manhattan, enabling new roasters and trading firms to come in. By the 18th century, the Coffee District was created. By 1876, the U.S. was importing over a third of the world’s coffee, much of which came through New York Ports. Since then, New York has become a hub for coffee lovers around the world. Many of the world's oldest coffee merchants are still active in New York and are open to the public for enjoyment.

Gilles Coffee is the oldest coffee merchant in all of the United States. Established in 1840, the merchant operated on Washington Street and now is housed in Brooklyn. Pictured above is the company's logo.

Many of the largest trends within coffee also started in New York. For instance, America's first espresso machine can be located at Cafe Reggio in Greenwich Village, New York. Espressos quickly became common across coffee shops in New York after this introduction.
